The police must push forward digital innovation in every field of policing to respond to the remote civilization brought earlier by COVID-19. The Korean Police have already opened a new horizon of digital policing by expanding its remote, online services. The police capacity on the field will be notably enhanced by adopting cutting-edge technologies such as AI and Big-data.

President Moon Jae-in at the 75th National Police Day Ceremony (21st October 2020)

Background

The coronavirus pandemic, which struck the entire world, has changed our daily lives drastically. The new remote lifestyle has kept us inside our homes, and the fear of the spreading virus has made us close our borders. Ironically, this situation with technology development leads our life radius to become broader than ever. The super-connected and highly sophisticated society has come earlier than we anticipated due to the pandemic. In this 'new normal' era, a menace can be drawn upon humanity at all times.

While the virus impedes contact with others and cross-border exchanges, it does not mean the importance of policing or the necessity of international police cooperation has faltered. The commitment of the police has been ceaseless so far despite new challenges brought by the pandemic. For instance, the police are facing a complex request to maintain a balance between civil rights and public health in the community lockdowns, the banishment of private gatherings, and privacy infringement for epidemiology investigation. Emerging crimes, such as fake news in social media, online scams, illegal trafficking on the dark web, requires the police to advance digital police innovation using big data, artificial intelligence, the Internet of Things (IoT), drones, blockchain, and more.

In the dawn of this new era, when our world is struggling to recover from the devastating aftermath of COVID-19, sharing best practices and lessons learned are essential to overcome the emerging threats. The Korean National Police Agency (KNPA) plans to seek global solidarity and cooperation by hosting a meeting for police leaders worldwide to consider a new proactive and preventative policing paradigm. The host intends that the 2021 International Police Summit (IPS) in Seoul build a consensus among the participating global police leaders on defining emerging threats and developing resilience strategies in law enforcement.

History

The Korean National Police Agency (KNPA) hosted two international events gathering ministers, vice-ministers, and chiefs of police from 17 countries in 2015 and 28 countries and international organizations in 2019, respectively. The first meeting focused on bilateral cooperation among participants, while the second one opened discussions on new challenges to international police cooperation in a multilateral forum.

Taking over the success of the previous events, the 2021 IPS in Seoul will provide participants with a unique opportunity to build a network, identify better ways to secure public safety and security, and recognize the need for international police cooperation.

Objectives

The summit will provide ministers, vice-ministers, chiefs, and commissioners of the police with opportunities to:

  • 1. Define the new threats in the 'new normal' era and share various views on resilience strategy to address the challenges;
  • 2. Build a friendship with key decision-makers in police organizations around the world;
  • 3. Share experiences and efforts of crisis response and innovative police initiatives in each country;
  • 4. Discuss how to promote police cooperation in specific areas of interest with participating police leaders; and
  • 5. Have various experiences to understand Korean culture.

Expected outcomes
The 2021 IPS in Seoul is expected to develop;

  • 1. A common understanding of changing policing environment and new threats to police organizations;
  • 2. Combined ideas on comprehensive resilience strategy for police organizations in the coronavirus pandemic;
  • 3. A note of the host containing detailed discussion during the summit and a way forward for sustainable cooperation among the participants; and
  • 4. A strategic partnership among the participating organizations.
