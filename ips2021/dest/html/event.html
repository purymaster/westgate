International Police Summit in Seoul, 2019

The second IPS took place at Shilla Hotel in Seoul, Korea, from 20th to 23rd October 2019. The theme of the meeting was 'Global Partnership for Smart Policing.' The event was participated by 103 police leaders and officers from 28 police organizations. The participants included:

  • Two ministers from the Russian Federation and the Republic of Uzbekistan;
  • The United Nations Assistant-Secretary-General for Rule of Law and Security Institutions;
  • Two vice-ministers from the People's Republic of China and the Socialist Republic of Vietnam;
  • Five national or federal police heads from Angola, Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Mongolia, and Myanmar;
  • Eight deputy heads from Fiji, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Rwanda, South Africa, Turkey, and the United Arab Emirates; and
  • Nine Director-Generals from Cambodia, Canada, Malaysia, New Zealand, Oman, the Philippines, Singapore, Uganda, and the United States.

At the plenary meeting, the participants shared their views on promoting international discussion on policing and law enforcement. They suggested that the global multi-lateral summits should discuss security and policing issues more often, and sub-committees should be established on international police cooperation. Using technology for smart policing was essential, and countries should share their experiences and knowledge in this regard. Participants also had bilateral talks on investigation cooperation, police training and capacity building, and infrastructure development in law enforcement. They also agreed to do more on deporting criminals and protecting foreign nationals. The delegations also visited the Korea Police World Expo, where they showed much interest in technology and equipment for smart policing.

International Police Summit 2015

The first summit for global police leaders was held at multiple places in Seoul, Korea, from 19th to 23rd October 2015. The main theme of the event was 'Together for a safer world.' 70 police leaders and officers from 16 countries and Interpol came to Korea to attend the meeting. The participants were:

  • Ministers from China and Uzbekistan;
  • Vice ministers from Indonesia and Vietnam;
  • Heads of the Police from Guatemala, Mongolia, Papua New Guinea, Sri Lanka, Tunisia, and the United Arab Emirates;
  • Executive Director of the Interpol;
  • Deputy heads from Cambodia, Hong Kong, Jordan, and the Philippines; and/li>
  • Director Generals from Japan, Thailand, and Qatar.

The event was mainly composed of bilateral meetings between participating delegates and the Korean Police leadership to discuss current critical issues. Besides, the Korean Police introduced its 'K-Cop Wave Program' to support other countries in building police capacity. The Participants visited Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency to see its 112 police dispatch system and traffic information center. A police equipment exhibition took place at the Agency’s mobile police headquarters. The event closed with a cultural experience and farewell dinner at Sebit island on Han River.
