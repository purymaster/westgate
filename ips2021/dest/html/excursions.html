Other Schedule

Photo provided by Live Studio - Korea Tourism Organization

Insa-dong

Insa-dong, located in the heart of the city, is an important place where old but precious traditional goods are on display. There is one main road in Insa-dong with alleys on each side. Within these alleys are galleries, traditional restaurants, teahouses, and cafes.

Website : http://www.insainfo.or.kr
Distance : 1.1km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by IR Studio - Korea Tourism Organization

Namsan Seoultower

The tower has become the number one tourist attraction for domestic tourists as Koreans are attracted to its unique structure and the view of Seoul it offers from its great height. With increasing number of TV shows and dramas filmed at the tower, thanks to the recent trend of the Korean wave, the Tower has become a famous tourist destination frequented by both domestic and international tourists.

Website : https://www.seoultower.co.kr/en/
Distance : 2.1km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by 두드림 - Korea Tourism Organization

Gyeongbokgung Palace

It was in 1395, three years after the Joseon Dynasty was founded by King Taejo (Yi Seong-gye), when the construction of the main royal Palace was completed after the capital of the newly founded dynasty moved from Gaeseong to Seoul (then known as Hanyang). With Mount Bugaksan to its rear and Mount Namsan in the foreground, the site of Gyeongbokgung Palace was at the heart of Seoul and, indeed, deemed auspicious according to the traditional practice of geomancy.

Website : http://www.royalpalace.go.kr:8080/html/eng_gbg/main/main.jsp
Distance : 2.4km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by IR Studio - Korea Tourism Organization

Kwangjang Market

The name of Kwangjang Market is from the meaning of “Keep after collecting widely.”
It is a great honor to have a market with 100 years of tradition and history in Korea.
Foods and Sense of hometown. It’s all here.

Website : http://www.kwangjangmarket.co.kr/en/
Distance : 3.3km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by 김지호 - Korea Tourism Organization

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon, located north of Chenggyecheon Stream and Jongno, embodies the spirit of out Korean ancestors in its 600-year-old urban history. Located between Gyeongbokgung Palace and Changdeokgung Palace, Bukchon is a historic site that was a residential area of aristocrats during the Joseon period. The area is home to five Historic Sites, four Seoul Folklore Heritages, three Tangible Cultural Heritages, one Cultural Heritage Material, and various other historic properties including Boreum Well in Seokjeong, the Site of Gwanghyewon (“Widespread Relief House”), Han Yong-un’s House (one of Seoul’s Future Heritages), and the Former Korea Art Gallery building.

Website : https://hanok.seoul.go.kr/front/eng/index.do
Distance : 3.8km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by 이범수 - Korea Tourism Organization

The War Memorial of Korea

The War Memorial of Korea, located in Yongsan-gu, Seoul, exhibits and preserves materials related to the Korean War and serves as a national moral educational venue. It was established on June 10, 1994 by the War Memorial Service Korea Society to commemorate the noble sacrifice of patriotic martyrs. The museum houses approximately 33,000 artifacts with about 10,000 on display at indoor and outdoor exhibitions.

Website : https://www.warmemo.or.kr/LNG/main.do?lan=en
Distance : 4.2km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by 김지호 - Korea Tourism Organization

National Museum of Korea

Visitors can fully enjoy its six galleries in the Permanent Exhibition Hall arranged by period and theme, special exhibition galleries that feature various contents, diverse guided-tour programs to deepen one’s understanding, the Children's Museum where kids are able to enjoy and experiment with the five senses, a variety of educational programs, and immersible AR and VR contents using advanced IT technology.

Website : https://www.museum.go.kr/site/eng/home
Distance : 6.6km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by IR Studio - Korea Tourism Organization

Yeouido Hangang Park

Yeouido Hangang Park, the center of politics, finance, and media, is a popular destination for office workers and others because of its great public accessibility. Peach Blossom Festival, World Fireworks Festival, concerts, marathons, and other programs make Yeouido Hangang Park a place of relaxation and entertainment. There is an eco-friendly park as well as an educational center that focuses on instilling the value of preserving nature.

Website : https://seoulforest.or.kr/english
Distance : 7.6km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by 이범수 - Korea Tourism Organization

Bongeunsa Temple

Bongeunsa is a Buddhist temple founded in 794 during the Silla (one of the Three Kingdoms of Korea) period. The temple has more than 1,000 years of history and has many interesting historic and cultural features, including woodblock carvings of the Avatamsaka Sutra (Flower Garland Sutra). The temple is also a very popular tourist destination, offering various programs relating to traditional Korean Buddhist culture, in a variety of languages.

Website : http://www.bongeunsa.org:90/
Distance : 11.8km *From the IPS venue
Photo provided by 김승래 - Korea Tourism Organization

Lotte World Tower

The Seoul Sky Observatory is located at the top of the Lotte World Tower, the world's fifth tallest building standing 123 stories and 555 meters high. As the tallest building in South Korea, it is the only place where you can take in a gorgeous 360-degree view of Seoul, the capital city of South Korea roaring with brilliant history and dynamic modern culture.

Website : https://seoulsky.lotteworld.com/en/main/index.do?lang=en
Distance : 16.2km *From the IPS venue
